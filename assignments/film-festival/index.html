About the CONS Film Festival

The aim of the CONS Film Festival is to showcase the best Classic, Obscure, and New films, along with original submissions. Taking place every May this film festival bases it's selection of films on a different genre annually.

The film festival in Galway was recognised by the International Federation of Film Producers Association, one of only five globally to be approved at their 1950 Congress in Cannes. In 2020, CONS Film Festival is one of a handful of film festivals in Ireland to host three awards with Oscar® Accreditation

Check below to see this year's movie selections, the showing time and dates, to find out more about submitting a movie of your own, or volunteering at our festival.

Upcoming Festival

The CONS Film Festival will be taking place from the 20th to the 23rd of May and we are happy to announce that this year's festival's theme is 'Thriller'. Submissions featuring other minor themes are more than welcome, so long as thriller is the main genre.

Films Submissions

Shadows

  • Thriller
  • Drama

Alma and Alex are two teenage sisters who are coming of age within their confined and surreal world. The pair have lived with their controlling mother in an abandoned woodland hotel for their entire lives. As they attempt to survive in this sheltered settlement, their mother constantly warns them of the perils of the outside world, principally the life-threatening danger of sunlight. And so, the family only leave their dwelling at night, under the safety of the shadows.

Black Medicine

  • Thriller
  • Suspense

Black Medicine is a suspenseful thriller, set in the heart of the Irish crime underbelly, starring Amybeth McNulty, Orla Brady, and Antonia Campbell-Hughes. Jo is a black-market medic who carries out illegal operations for the criminal underworld. When she gives refuge to a young girl, she must choose between breaking her medical oath or crossing her ruthless employers. Black Medicine was written and directed by Screen International Star of Tomorrow 2020 winner Colum Eastwood.

Submissions Info

The CONS Film Festival welcomes submissions of features and shorts that align with the festival's annual theme.

  1. A feature film is considered to be 52 or more minutes in length, and a short film is anything less.
  2. Submission fees and deadlines:
    Early bird deadline: Friday 10th December, 2021 at 5p.m. G.M.T. €30 entry fee.
    Regular deadline: Friday 18th February, 2022 at 5p.m. G.M.T. €40 entry fee.
    Late deadline: Monday 14th March, 2022 at 5p.m. G.M.T. €50 entry fee.
  3. All languages of film are accepted. Submissions and screeners of non-English language films must contain English subtitles.
  4. Send any physical submission materials to:
    CONS Film Submissions,

    Galway,
    H91 TYC9,
    Ireland

Our Logo

The creation of our logo was based on the four aspects of the CONS Film Festival: Classic, Obscure, New, and Submissions.

The classic aspect is represented by using Ireland's classic colour, green. The obscure aspect is represented by the logo itself.

The new aspect is represented by the spiral and loop part of the logo which is taken from the top half of the celtic symbol for new beginnings.

And lastly,the submissions aspect is represented in the lower half of the logo with two abstract arrows.
